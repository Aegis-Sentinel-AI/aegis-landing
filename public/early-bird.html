🛒 How to Buy $AEGIS — Step by Step

The Early Bird presale runs directly on Arbitrum One through a verified smart contract. You send ETH, the contract records your allocation on-chain, and your tokens become claimable on listing day. Here's exactly how it works:

1

Set Up a Web3 Wallet

You'll need a Web3 wallet like MetaMask, Rabby, or any WalletConnect-compatible wallet. If you don't have one, install MetaMask as a browser extension or mobile app — it takes about 2 minutes.

Important: Write down your seed phrase and store it securely offline. This is the only way to recover your wallet if you lose access. Never share it with anyone.
2

Add the Arbitrum One Network

$AEGIS lives on Arbitrum One (Chain ID: 42161), an Ethereum Layer 2 network. Your wallet needs to be connected to this network to interact with the presale contract.

Quick add: Visit chainlist.org/chain/42161 and click "Add to MetaMask" — it auto-configures the network settings for you. Or add manually: RPC https://arb1.arbitrum.io/rpc, Chain ID 42161, Symbol ETH.
3

Get ETH on Arbitrum One

You need ETH on the Arbitrum network to buy tokens and pay gas fees (which are very low — typically under $0.10). There are two ways to get ETH on Arbitrum:

Option A — Bridge from Ethereum: If you already hold ETH on Ethereum mainnet, use the official Arbitrum Bridge to move it to Arbitrum One. Bridging takes around 10 minutes.
Option B — Buy directly: Use an exchange that supports Arbitrum withdrawals (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken, Bybit) and withdraw ETH directly to your Arbitrum One wallet address. This avoids bridging entirely.
4

Send ETH to the Presale Contract

Once you have ETH on Arbitrum, simply send it to the presale contract address below. The smart contract automatically calculates your $AEGIS allocation at the current rate of 1 ETH ≈ 133,333 AEGIS (based on the $0.015 price and current ETH price). Your allocation is recorded on-chain immediately.

How it works on-chain: When the contract receives your ETH, it emits a Purchase event containing your wallet address and token allocation. You can verify your purchase instantly on Arbiscan. There is no minimum purchase — any amount of ETH works.
5

Wait for Listing Day — January 1, 2027

Your tokens are locked in the presale contract until the Token Generation Event (TGE) on January 1, 2027. On that date, token trading begins on Uniswap and Camelot DEX at the listing price of $0.040, and your tokens become claimable.

No action needed until then. Your allocation is safely stored in the smart contract. On listing day, you'll call the claim() function (or we'll provide a simple claim page) and your $AEGIS tokens will be sent directly to your wallet. Early Bird buyers effectively bought at 62.5% below the listing price.

🔷 Why Arbitrum One?

You might wonder why $AEGIS is on Arbitrum instead of Ethereum mainnet. Arbitrum One is an Ethereum Layer 2 — it inherits Ethereum's security while offering dramatically lower fees and faster transactions. Here's why we chose it:

⚡

Near-Zero Gas Fees

Transactions on Arbitrum cost $0.01–$0.10 instead of $5–$50+ on Ethereum mainnet. This means buying $AEGIS costs almost nothing in fees.

🔒

Ethereum Security

Arbitrum is a "rollup" — all transactions are ultimately verified and settled on Ethereum L1. Your tokens have the same security guarantees as Ethereum mainnet.

🏎️

Fast Confirmations

Transactions confirm in about 1 second on Arbitrum, compared to 12–15 seconds on Ethereum. Your purchase is confirmed almost instantly.

🌐

Largest L2 Ecosystem

Arbitrum has the highest TVL of any L2 (~$18B+). Major DeFi protocols like Uniswap, Aave, and GMX are all here — meaning deep liquidity for $AEGIS at listing.

🔗

Same Wallet, Same ETH

You use the same wallet address and same ETH as Ethereum mainnet. No new token to buy — just switch your wallet to the Arbitrum network.

🤖

Built for AI Security

Our AI engine anchors threat verdicts on-chain. Low fees on Arbitrum make it viable to write thousands of security proofs daily — something impossible on L1.
